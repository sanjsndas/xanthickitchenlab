📜 Privacy Policy

✅ Effective date: January 15, 2026 | Last updated: March 1, 2026
Xanthic Kitchen Lab ("we", "us", "our") operates the website xanthickitchenlab.example and related services. This page informs you of our policies regarding collection, use, and disclosure of personal data when you use our Site and the choices you have associated with that data.
⚖️ Transparency First – Molecular Gastronomy Pledge

1. Information We Collect

While using our website, we may ask you to provide certain personally identifiable information that can be used to contact or identify you (“Personal Data”). This may include, but is not limited to:

  • Email address – when you contact us via our contact form, request a workshop, or sign up for the Hydrocolloid Bootcamp newsletter.
  • First name and last name – to personalize our communication regarding xanthan gum recipes and lab updates.
  • Phone number – only if voluntarily provided for urgent consultation scheduling.
  • Message content – any details you share about culinary projects, modernist inquiries, or collaboration proposals.
  • Usage Data – we may collect information on how the Site is accessed and used (pages visited, time spent, referrer). This helps us improve our 57,800+ word resource library.

We do not collect sensitive data (health, genetic, biometric) nor any payment information — any future lab product purchases will be handled through secured third-party gateways not directly processed by us.

2. How We Use Your Data

Xanthic Kitchen Lab uses the collected data for various purposes:

  • To respond to your inquiries, provide molecular gastronomy support, and share recipe clarifications.
  • To send you occasional updates about new xanthan techniques, lab events, or special workshops (only with explicit consent).
  • To improve and personalize your experience on our website — including content recommendations based on popular hydrocolloid topics.
  • To monitor the usage and technical performance of our digital lab (anonymized analytics).
  • To detect, prevent and address technical issues or security incidents.

We never sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties for marketing purposes. Ever. Your trust is the main ingredient of our kitchen.

3. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR compliance)

If you are from the European Economic Area (EEA), our legal basis for collecting and using the personal information described in this Privacy Policy depends on the Personal Data we collect and the specific context in which we collect it. We may process your data because:

  • You have given us permission to do so (e.g., by submitting our contact form).
  • The processing is necessary for the performance of a contract or to take steps at your request before entering a contract (e.g., scheduling a paid workshop).
  • Processing is necessary for compliance with our legal obligations.
  • Processing is necessary for legitimate interests pursued by Xanthic Kitchen Lab (e.g., improving our gastronomy content, protecting against fraud).

4. Data Retention & Security

We retain your Personal Data only for as long as is necessary for the purposes set out in this Privacy Policy. Contact form submissions are stored for a maximum of 24 months to maintain continuity of ongoing culinary consultations, after which they are anonymized or deleted. Usage data is generally retained for shorter periods except when used to enhance site performance.

We implement reasonable security measures including encrypted connections (HTTPS), access controls, and routine vulnerability scans. However, no method of transmission over the Internet is 100% secure — while we strive to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

5. Sharing of Information

We may share your personal data only in the following limited scenarios:

  • Service providers: We may employ third-party companies to facilitate our website (e.g., analytics via privacy-friendly Plausible or similar) — these parties only have access to perform tasks on our behalf and are obligated not to disclose or use it for any other purpose.
  • Legal requirements: If required by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities (e.g., a court order).
  • Business transfer: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, your Personal Data may be transferred. We will notify you before your data becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

We do not share your information with any advertising networks or data brokers.

6. Your Data Protection Rights (incl. CCPA & GDPR)

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure (“right to be forgotten”): Request deletion of your personal data, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Restrict processing: Request that we limit how we use your data.
  • Data portability: Receive a structured, machine-readable copy of your data.
  • Object to processing: Opt-out of certain uses (e.g., marketing emails).

To exercise any of these rights, please contact our Data Protection Gastronomist at privacy@xanthickitchen.example or via the contact page. We will respond within 30 days. No fee for legitimate requests.

For California residents: We do not sell your personal information (as defined by the CCPA). You have the right to opt-out of any future “sale” of data – though we don’t engage in such practices. You may request information about categories of personal data collected.

7. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance user experience and analyze site traffic. Cookies are small files stored on your device. You can instruct your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent. However, some parts of our site may not function properly without cookies.

We use strictly necessary cookies (for navigation) and analytical cookies to count visits and traffic sources, so we can measure and improve the performance of our molecular gastronomy library. No third-party advertising cookies are deployed. For detailed cookie information, please see our Cookies Policy.

8. Children's Privacy

Our Service does not address anyone under the age of 13 (“Children”). We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from anyone under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and you are aware that your Child has provided us with Personal Data, please contact us. If we become aware that we have collected Personal Data from children without verification of parental consent, we take steps to remove that information from our servers.

9. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to other sites (e.g., scientific references, ingredient suppliers). If you click on a third-party link, you will be directed to that site. We strongly advise you to review the Privacy Policy of every site you visit. We have no control over and assume no responsibility for the content, privacy policies, or practices of any third-party sites or services.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the “effective date” at the top. You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes. Changes to this Privacy Policy are effective when they are posted on this page.

Significant modifications will be communicated via a notice on our homepage or by email to our registered contacts. Continued use of the Site after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, wish to exercise your rights, or want to request a copy of your data, please reach out:

  • Email: privacy@xanthickitchen.example (or lab@xanthickitchen.example for general contact)

Xanthic Kitchen Lab is based in the United States. If you are accessing our site from outside the US, please be aware that your information may be transferred to, stored, and processed in the US where our servers are located. By using our website, you consent to this transfer. We take steps to ensure adequate safeguards are in place consistent with this Privacy Policy.
