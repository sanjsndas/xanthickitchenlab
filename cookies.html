1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. They allow the website to recognize your device, remember your actions and preferences (such as language, font size, or previously visited recipe pages) over a period of time, so you don’t have to keep re-entering them. Cookies also help us understand how visitors interact with our 57,800+ word gastronomy archive, enabling us to refine and optimize our xanthan gum guides.

We use both session cookies (which expire when you close your browser) and persistent cookies (which remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them).

2. How We Use Cookies – Categories & Purpose

Xanthic Kitchen Lab uses only strictly necessary and analytical/performance cookies. We do NOT deploy any advertising, tracking, or cross-site marketing cookies. Your culinary data stays within our lab.

3. Your Consent & Control – Cookie Management

When you first visit Xanthic Kitchen Lab, a cookie banner informs you about our use of cookies. By continuing to use the site or clicking “Accept all” you agree to our cookie settings as described. You can also manage preferences via our built-in preference center (see below).

⚙️ Manage your cookie preferences anytime:
Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can block or delete cookies entirely, but please note that blocking strictly necessary cookies may impair site functionality (e.g., contact forms, login). Below are quick links for major browsers:
  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Manage cookies

Alternatively, you can disable non-essential analytics cookies via our Cookie Preferences widget (bottom-right corner of the site).

4. Third-Party Cookies & External Services

We embed no third-party advertising or social media plugins. However, we may occasionally include links to external scientific references or ingredient suppliers. Those third-party websites may set their own cookies when you click through — we have no control over those cookies and encourage you to review their privacy policies. Xanthic Kitchen Lab does not integrate Facebook Pixel, Google Ads, or any retargeting services.

For our analytics, we use an anonymized, cookieless fallback option when possible: we collect minimal usage data without persistent identifiers. Our priority is protecting your digital footprint while improving our xanthan gum database.

5. Duration & Storage of Cookie Information

Session cookies last only as long as your browser is open. Persistent cookies are stored for the periods described in the table above (max 14 months for analytics, and up to 30 days for strictly necessary preferences). You can manually delete cookies at any time through your browser settings. After deletion, some preferences (like cookie consent choices) may reset and require re-selection.

6. How We Protect Cookie Data

All cookie data is transmitted over secure HTTPS connections. Any analytics data is aggregated and anonymized, meaning we cannot identify individual users. Strictly necessary cookies contain only session identifiers or CSRF tokens — no personal information such as email or name is stored in cookies. We follow data minimization principles in line with our Privacy Policy.
